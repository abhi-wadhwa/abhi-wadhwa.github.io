Random Matrix Theory for High-Dimensional Sub-Gaussian Models

Exploration of the Spielman–Teng Conjecture through spectral analysis.

2025

USC Department of Mathematics

Exploration of the Spielman–Teng Conjecture through spectral analysis of sub-Gaussian random matrices, focusing on denoising applications and efficient eigenvalue estimation techniques for high-dimensional modeling.

Focus

  • BBP transitions & detectability thresholds
  • Sub-Gaussian tails & non-asymptotic bounds
  • Eigenvector delocalization & bias control

Methods

  • Marchenko–Pastur & Tracy–Widom regimes
  • Shrinkage covariance & spiked inference
  • Monte-Carlo & finite-sample diagnostics

Artifacts

  • Prototype spectral denoiser (Python)
  • Benchmarks on synthetic & public data
  • Notes on practical spectral thresholds

Applications

  • Risk de-noising for portfolios
  • Pre-whitening for ML pipelines

Measure-Theoretic Foundations for Stochastic Control & MDPs

Foundations for martingale theory and optimal control in Machine Learning.

2024

USC Department of Mathematics

*Measure Theory and Fine Proprties of Functions* by Evans, emphasizing $\sigma$-algebras, integration, and convergence theorems as the foundation for martingale theory in stochastic processes, with applications to optimal control and Markov decision processes for Machine Learning.

Focus

  • Borel MDPs & measurable selectors
  • Contractions of Bellman operators
  • Average- vs. discounted-cost criteria

Methods

  • Martingales & optional stopping
  • Fixed-point theorems
  • Ergodicity/recurrence conditions

Artifacts

  • Clean convergence proofs (notes)
  • Reference guide on measurability subtleties
  • Small-state MDP demos

Applications

  • Safe RL policy regularity
  • Operations & finance control

Convex Optimization for Multi-Objective Dynamic Policy Systems

Casting intertemporal policy trade-offs as constrained convex programs.

2024

USC Marshall School of Business

Casting intertemporal policy trade-offs as constrained convex programs with equilibrium consistency; deriving implementable rules with interpretable multipliers and sensitivity analysis.

Focus

  • Pareto frontiers & welfare weights
  • Time-coupled feasibility constraints
  • Shadow prices as policy signals

Methods

  • KKT & duality; saddle-point analysis
  • Primal–dual & projected-gradient schemes
  • Robustness & sensitivity

Artifacts

  • Prototype solver for dynamic convex programs
  • Trade-off visualizations & dual dynamics
  • Write-up on multiplier interpretability

Applications

  • Dynamic regulation with multiple targets
  • Mechanism design & allocation
