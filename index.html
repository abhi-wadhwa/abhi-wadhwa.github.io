Howdy! I’m Abhi and I study Applied Mathematics at USC on Scholarship.

I build models for decision-making under uncertainty using probability, optimization, and strategic interaction. Current work spans high-dimensional noise filtering with random matrix methods, stochastic control for Markov decision processes, and spatial alignment for large datasets. Previously, I’ve worked on spatial alignment models at the World Bank, theoretical machine learning (including transformer architectures), and policy analysis on inequality economics.

At the moment, I’m most interested in the intersection of machine learning, game theory, and optimization, particularly in developing algorithms that formalize reasoning and rational decision-making. My current work focuses on how principles from utility theory, strategic interaction, and iterative optimization can inform learning systems that reason about incentives, uncertainty, and trade-offs. Broadly, I’m fascinated by how game-theoretic and mathematical frameworks can advance interpretable, multi-agent AI systems capable of exhibiting strategic and goal-directed behavior.
